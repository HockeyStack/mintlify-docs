## Overview

Stakeholder Discovery automatically identifies and maps key contacts at your target accounts based on your configured personas. Discovered contacts appear in each company's **People** tab with email addresses, LinkedIn profiles, and employment status.

## How it works

When stakeholder discovery runs for a company:

1. The system evaluates your persona definitions (department, seniority, title keywords).
2. For each persona, it searches for matching contacts at the company.
3. Discovered contacts are enriched with email addresses and LinkedIn profiles.
4. Results appear in the company's **People** tab (see [People](/sales-agents/people)).

Discovery runs automatically as part of the overnight background worker. You can also trigger it manually.

## Running discovery for a sales view

You can trigger stakeholder discovery for all companies in a specific sales view from the sales dashboard.

1. Navigate to a **sales view** on the sales dashboard.
2. Open the **configuration dropdown** for the view.
3. Select **Generate Stakeholder Maps**.
4. The system discovers contacts across all companies in the view based on your persona definitions.

This maps stakeholders in bulk — the system uses your existing journey data alongside new contact searches to build a comprehensive picture of each account's key people.

## Running discovery for a single company

To discover or refresh contacts at a specific company:

1. Navigate to the company's detail page.
2. Open the **People** tab.
3. Click the **Re-generate** button.
4. Select which persona to discover contacts for.
5. New contacts appear as they are discovered and enriched.

A progress indicator shows discovery status. Use this to test persona definitions on individual companies before running discovery across a full view.

## Best practices

* **Start narrow, then expand.** Run discovery on a single company first using the People tab. Review the results to verify your personas produce the right contacts before running across a full view.
* **Check email coverage.** If many discovered contacts show "Enriching" or "No email" status, your personas may be targeting roles that are harder to find email addresses for.
* **Revisit after persona changes.** When you edit personas, re-generate on a few companies to verify the changes produce better results before the next discovery cycle processes your full pipeline.

## Constraints

* Stakeholder discovery depends on persona quality. If no personas are configured, no stakeholders are discovered.
* Discovery results depend on available public data. Some contacts may not be found if they have limited online presence.
* Running discovery across a large sales view triggers processing for many companies. The first full run may take time to complete.
* Discovered stakeholders are preserved — re-running discovery adds new contacts without removing existing ones (unless they are flagged as having left).

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title={"How often does discovery run automatically?"}>
  The overnight background worker processes companies daily. You can also trigger discovery manually per company (from the People tab) or per view (from the sales dashboard configuration dropdown).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title={"What happens when I add a new persona?"}>
  New personas take effect on the next discovery cycle. Companies are re-evaluated, and contacts matching the new persona are added. Existing contacts are not removed.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title={"Can I remove a discovered stakeholder?"}>
  Stakeholders are managed by the system based on persona matches. To stop discovering certain types of contacts, adjust your persona definitions (narrow the department, seniority, or add exclude keywords).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title={"What is the difference between generating from the People tab vs. the sales view?"}>
  The People tab re-generates contacts for a single company. The sales view option runs discovery across all companies in that view. Use the People tab for testing and iteration, and the sales view option for bulk discovery.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title={"Does re-generating remove existing contacts?"}>
  No. Re-generating adds new contacts and updates existing ones. Contacts who have left the company are flagged but not removed.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
